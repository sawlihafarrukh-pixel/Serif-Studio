# Tadabbur Journal — Product Notes

A guided journal for reflecting deeply on the words of the Quran. Part of **The Afternoon Club** brand (see `../Afternoon Club/afternoon-club-brand-notes.md`).

## Product description (for store listings)

Recommended structure: lead with the emotional hook, then follow with the practical detail.

> Some ayahs stop you in your tracks. They sit with you long after you've closed the Quran. The Tadabbur Journal was made for those moments — a quiet space to slow down, reflect, and let the words of Allah truly reach your heart.
>
> Each page walks you through recording your chosen ayah, its translation, your tafseer notes, personal reflections, duas, and action steps — helping you move from simply reading to truly understanding and living by what you read. Designed for the Muslim who wants to build a more intentional and meaningful relationship with the Quran, one ayah at a time.

Shorter alternative:

> A journal for those who want to go deeper with the Quran. The Tadabbur Journal gives you dedicated space to reflect on each ayah — its meaning, its lessons, and how to carry it into your daily life.

## Interior page flow

Each reflection spread follows a complete arc:

Surah → Ayah → Translation → Tafseer → Dua → Action Plan

Moves the reader from reading to understanding to acting.

## Inside cover page

Keep it minimal — a quiet breath before the journal begins. Match the restrained energy of the interior pages.

Include:
- A **bismillah** — centred, Arabic calligraphy or clean Arabic text
- A short intention-setting line, e.g. "Begin with an open heart and a willing mind." / "This journal belongs to someone seeking closeness with Allah."
- A "This journal belongs to: ____" line (gives it a gifted-book feel)
- A small, subtle Afternoon Club mark at the bottom

Design feel: cream / off-white background, one small illustrative element (a tiny sun motif or single botanical), lots of white space, elegant lightweight font. Avoid over-decorating.

## Last page

Recommended: **About the journal** page followed by a **closing reflection**.
- About page — a short paragraph on what tadabbur means and the purpose behind the journal (personal, and subtly markets the creator)
- Closing — a beautiful ayah or hadith styled as a quote page, e.g. "May Allah grant you the ability to reflect on His words, act upon them, and find peace in their meaning." with name/brand underneath

Other options considered: free "My Notes" pages; an intention-setting page that mirrors the opening.

## Pricing

Digital download market range: $5–$15 USD. Launch at **$5–$7** as a new seller, with an "introductory price for the first two weeks" framing to justify a later raise. Raise to $10–$15 once there are reviews, more pages, or a bundle.

Bundle idea: Tadabbur Journal + Dear Allah Diary together for ~$10 — lifts perceived value immediately.

## Physical vs digital

Audience is expected to prefer **physical copies**. Options:
- **Print-on-demand** (Printify+Etsy, Lulu Direct, Amazon KDP) — no inventory risk
- **Small local print run** (20–50 copies) sold via Instagram drops — better margins, matches past Pretty Oddinary experience. Printing in Pakistan is cheaper than Malaysia.

Test demand before printing a batch.
