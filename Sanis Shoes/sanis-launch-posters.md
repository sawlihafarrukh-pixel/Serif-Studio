# Sanis Shoes — Three Launch Posters

Three poster concepts for Sanis, designed to work across IG, email, web hero, and print (A3/A2). Each poster includes copy, a visual brief, image-generation prompts for **nano banana** and **ChatGPT** (DALL-E 3 / gpt-image-1), and step-by-step Canva instructions for compositing the typography.

---

## Universal Rules (apply to all three)

- **Palette is sealed.** Only `#B4A08E` warm taupe, `#3C2B24` dark brown, `#F6F2EE` off-white, `#C9A3A3` dusty blush. No rust, no greens, no saturated colours.
- **No faces, necks, or chests. Ever.** Hands and ankles permitted. If a figure appears with the shoe, it must be **fully clothed in modest dress** — long sleeves, wide-leg jeans or long skirts, no exposed skin above the elbow. Frame **strictly below the chest, ideally below the knees or thighs.**
- **Typography:** The Seasons for display, Montserrat for body / CTAs / dates. Both available in Canva (The Seasons requires Pro). Sentence case — never all-caps in poster copy.
- **No exclamation marks. No emojis.** Em-dashes and periods carry the rhythm.
- **Generate the base image WITHOUT text.** Both nano banana and ChatGPT render text as gibberish at poster scale. Composite all type in Canva afterwards.
- **Canvas:** 1200 × 1800 px (2:3 portrait) as the working size — scales cleanly to A3 (3508 × 4961 @ 300dpi) and A2 (4961 × 7016 @ 300dpi) for print. For square IG variants, crop centre to 1080 × 1080.
- **Light:** late-afternoon Karachi window light, raking directional, no flash, no presets.

### Path B — Use the actual product image (RECOMMENDED)

You have a clean photo of the cream-taupe Sanis ballet flats (`Screenshot 2026-06-06 at 4.22.28 PM.png` — a top-down pair on yellow background). Skip the image-gen prompts above and follow this Canva flow:

1. **Open the product photo** in a new Canva design and **remove the background** — *Edit photo → BG remover*. The yellow background drops out cleanly.
2. **Use only one shoe** — duplicate the cut-out, hide one. Keep the right shoe in profile-friendly view; delete the left from the layer panel for this poster (Poster 2 is one-shoe, not a pair).
3. **New 1200 × 1800 canvas** — background fill `#F6F2EE` (off-white) for the cleanest version. For a moodier version, fill `#B4A08E` (warm taupe).
4. **Place a "marble plate"** — drop in a 1200 × 720 strip of cream-veined marble (search Canva for "cream marble texture") anchored to the bottom of the canvas. This becomes the surface the shoe sits on.
5. **Place the cut-out shoe** — centred horizontally, anchored at ~55% from top. Rotate slightly (~7° tilt) so the toe points down-left for a more dynamic three-quarter feel. Shoe should occupy ~50% of canvas height.
6. **Drop shadow** — soft drop shadow on the cut-out: offset 0 / 50, blur 90, opacity 22%, colour `#3C2B24`. This mimics the raking-light shadow the original photo lost when you cut it out.
7. **Colour-correct the shoe** — Canva's *Adjust* panel: warmth +10, tint +3, brightness −4. The original photo is bright and slightly cool; you want a warmer, slightly deeper read so it sits cohesively with the cream marble.
8. **Optional muslin prop** — top-left corner, drop in a small (200 × 250 px) crop of folded off-white muslin / linen texture to mirror the prop language of the IG launch sequence. Anchor at top-left, ~80 px in from each edge.

The interior insole gold *Sanis* script is barely visible at this angle — that's fine for Poster 2. Save the close-up insole detail shot for a separate IG carousel slide later (great content for the steady-state feed).

## Quick Reference

### Type sizes (for 1200 × 1800 canvas)

| Use | Font | Size | Tracking | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wordmark hero (Poster 1) | The Seasons | 280pt | −20 | 1.0 |
| Section headline (Poster 2) | The Seasons | 96pt | −10 | 1.05 |
| Tagline overlay (Poster 3) | The Seasons Italic | 88pt | 0 | 1.1 |

| Body line | Montserrat Regular | 24pt | 0 | 1.5 |
| CTA | Montserrat Medium | 22pt | +80 | 1.3 |
| Small wordmark | The Seasons | 32pt | 0 | 1.0 |
| Handle | Montserrat Medium | 20pt | +80 | 1.0 |

### Print scaling

For A3 print (3508 × 4961 @ 300dpi), multiply all type sizes by **2.92×**.
For A2 print (4961 × 7016 @ 300dpi), multiply all type sizes by **4.13×**.

Canva: design once at 1200 × 1800, then *Resize → Custom dimensions → 3508 × 4961* (Pro feature). Verify type sits cleanly after resize.

### Export specs

| Use | Format | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| IG carousel slide | PNG | 1080 × 1350 (4:5) — re-crop from poster |
| IG single post | PNG | 1080 × 1080 (square) — re-crop centre |
| Email banner | JPG | 1200 × 1800 (or 1600 × 800 horizontal crop) |
| Web hero | JPG | 1920 × 1080 (horizontal crop with copy repositioned) |
| Print A3 | PDF (CMYK) | 3508 × 4961 @ 300dpi |
| Print A2 | PDF (CMYK) | 4961 × 7016 @ 300dpi |

For print, convert RGB hex to CMYK before exporting:
- `#B4A08E` taupe → C18 M28 Y38 K8
- `#3C2B24` dark brown → C40 M60 Y65 K70
- `#F6F2EE` off-white → C3 M4 Y6 K0
- `#C9A3A3` dusty blush → C13 M37 Y28 K2

## Path B Universal — Working from Your Product Images

If you upload product photos and want to skip image generation for any of the three posters, the flow is:

1. **Send the image(s)** in chat — ideally on a plain background, daylight, in focus.
2. **I'll write you** a Canva-specific compositing brief — background swap, palette correction, shadow direction, where to crop, which poster slot it fits.
3. **In Canva** — *BG remover* on the product, place onto a brand-palette backdrop (taupe linen plate or off-white field), add the dropshadow recipe in the relevant poster section, and composite the typography per that poster's instructions.

The product images are most useful for **Poster 2 (Hero Product)**. Posters 1 and 3 are atmosphere-led — generated images suit those better unless you have a particularly cinematic still you want to lead with.
