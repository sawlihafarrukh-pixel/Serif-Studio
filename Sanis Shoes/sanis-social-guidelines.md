# Sanis Shoes — Social Media Guidelines & Launch Calendar

A book for running @sanisshoes on Instagram from day one. Strategy, rules of the feed, and the first nine posts — with image-generation prompts for nano banana.

---

## 1. Positioning

Sanis is a **Karachi-based family-run footwear maker**. The factory has been producing shoes for years — historically for bulk export — and is now launching its own brand: direct-to-consumer, quiet, considered. This Instagram launch is the public face of that rebrand. The production knowledge is not new; the brand is.

Sanis sits in white space no Pakistani footwear brand currently occupies. The reference set we studied — Bata PK, Servis, OneDegree, Michael Kors — runs hot, loud, face-led, and discount-driven. Sanis runs quiet.

The cold-start sequence behaves like a **fragrance house, not a shoe brand** — it builds heritage, intention, and material proof across the first seven posts before the brand asks for anything. Trust is earned through restraint, then the ask arrives gently — once on post 8, again on post 9.

**Tone:** quiet luxury, feminine, grounded.
**Origin:** Karachi. Family-run. Years of making.
**Etymology:** *Sanis*, from the Latin — whole, and sound.

---

## 1a. Actual Products & Colorway Plan

Based on real product photography from the factory (June 2026), the launch product range is:

1. **Lead colorway — palette-aligned.** Round-toe ballet flat in **soft warm cream-taupe nappa leather.** Smooth finish, lightly rounded throat, no heel. Interior insole is tan/camel with a gold script **Sanis** wordmark sitting in the heel cup — feature this in close-up shots when the angle allows. **Lead the launch sequence with this colorway.** It is the only product currently inside the brand palette.

2. **Secondary colorway — introduce post-launch-nine.** Round-toe ballet flat in **navy or near-black nubuck.** Same silhouette as the cream. Bring in roughly mid-July onwards, after the cream has established the brand mood. When shooting the darker colorway, keep all surrounding props palette-locked (warm-cream linen, taupe walls, off-white muslin) so the darker shoe reads as accent within the brand world rather than dominating the frame.

3. **Category extension — separate launch.** **Slate grey-blue suede slides / sandals** (open-toe, crossover straps). Different category from the flats. Save for a dedicated category launch a few weeks in. Do not mix into the launch nine.

The leather on the lead colorway reads slightly lighter than the palette's `#B4A08E` warm taupe — closer to a soft warm cream. The four-colour palette still governs the surrounding world; the shoe itself sits at the lighter end of the warm-cream-to-taupe range.

## 3. Voice Rules

- **Sentence case throughout.** Capitalize the first word of every line and all proper nouns. No all-caps in captions. No stylized capitalization mid-word.
- **Elegant restraint.** Short, considered, never chatty. Closer to fine-jewellery copy than text-message — think Maeven, Aesop, Diptyque.
- **One idea per caption.** Maximum two short lines. If it needs more, it belongs on a carousel slide, not in the caption.
- Every line readable in under ten seconds. Count it out loud before posting.
- **No exclamation marks. No emojis.** A period or an em-dash carries the sentence.
- Ban fashion clichés: *iconic, must-have, drop, cop, kicks, obsessed.*
- Ban brochure-poet clichés: *made slow, crafted to be felt, a shoe meant to be felt* — these are taglines, not captions.
- Soft CTAs only — "DM to reserve," "Link in bio." Never "Shop now," never a percent off.
- Anchor in Pakistani specificity without translating it — *Karachi, Clifton, Saddar, the sea* used as texture, never as costume.

---

## 4. Visual Rules

- **No faces, necks, or chests. Ever.** No portraits. If a figure appears with the shoe, it must be **fully clothed in modest dress** — long sleeves, wide-leg jeans or long skirts, no exposed skin above the elbow. Frame **strictly below the chest, ideally below the knees or thighs.** Hands, ankles, materials, environments, and packaging are all permitted.
- **Closed palette** — nothing outside the four colours appears in any frame. Production discipline only — never the message of a caption.
  - Warm taupe `#B4A08E`
  - Dark brown `#3C2B24`
  - Off-white `#F6F2EE`
  - Dusty blush `#C9A3A3`
- No rust orange — that's Serif Studio's accent, not Sanis. No greens. No saturated colours of any kind.
- **Off-white dominates roughly half of every frame** as negative space. The breathing room *is* the brand.
- **Dusty blush is accent only.** One object per frame max — a thread, a ceramic, a ribbon, a wall wash. Never decorative.
- **Natural light only.** Late-afternoon Karachi window light, raking directional. No flash. No hue-shifting presets. Exposure and contrast only.
- Four shot modes on rotation:
  1. **Tactile macro** (50mm+, shallow DoF)
  2. **Still-life** (one or two palette-locked props)
  3. **Environmental vignette** (real Karachi domestic and factory settings)
  4. **Below-frame human** — hands and ankles always; modestly-clothed figures framed below the chest (ideally below the knees or thighs) where needed. No jewellery, no nail polish, no styled forearms.
- **The Seasons** sets all display type. **Montserrat** for small captions, dates, prices. No other typefaces.
- If hands appear, they must be the **real hands of someone who has worked this factory for years** — calluses, age, real wear, henna where it appears naturally. Never aestheticised hand-model casting. Honesty over editorial.

## 5. Cadence & Posting Times

- **Frequency:** every 2–3 days during launch month, then every 3–4 days steady-state.
- **Time:** 20:00–21:00 MYT (8–9 pm Malaysia time). Best window from Serif Studio's existing data.
- **Vary by 15-minute increments** (20:00 / 20:15 / 20:30 / 20:45) across the week so the schedule doesn't read as bot-timed.
- **Day mix:** weekdays for evergreen, weekends for atmospheric / mood posts.

## 7. The Launch Arc

Posts **1–3** establish the world before the product — atmosphere, heritage, etymology.
Posts **4–7** walk inward through craft and material. The shoe arrives in pieces (stitch, last, sole) before it arrives whole. Full shoe is revealed only on post 6, single, like a relic.
Posts **8–9** are the only commerce gestures in the entire sequence — a packaging reveal with a soft *DM to reserve* on 8, and a threshold-and-pair closer on 9 that ties the nine posts into one quiet poem ending on permission to act.

## 9. Nano Banana Image Prompts

Nano banana (Gemini's image model) renders well from descriptive natural-language prompts. The system below uses a **master style preamble** that locks the look, plus a **per-post scene prompt** appended to it. Paste the preamble at the start of each generation, then add the post's scene prompt below it.

**Important caveat — typography:** nano banana will not perfectly render specific typefaces (The Seasons, Montserrat). For posts 3 and 7's end card, generate the image as a base composition and composite the actual type in Canva or Photoshop afterwards.

### Master Style Preamble (use for every image)

```
Editorial still-life photography in the style of fine fragrance houses
(Le Labo, Aesop, Diptyque) crossed with slow-fashion craft documentation.
Quiet luxury, feminine, grounded — Karachi-based Pakistani footwear maker
with years of production heritage, launching its own brand.

STRICT PALETTE — only these colours appear in the frame, nothing else:
- Warm taupe (#B4A08E) — linen, leather, lime-washed walls
- Deep dark brown (#3C2B24) — wood, polished leather, shadow
- Soft off-white (#F6F2EE) — muslin, marble, cream wall, dominant negative space
- Muted dusty blush (#C9A3A3) — single accent object only, max one per frame
No greens, no oranges, no rust, no saturated colours, no neons.

LIGHTING: natural late-afternoon Karachi window light only. Raking
directional from one side (typically camera left). Soft long shadows.
No flash. No harsh contrast. No colour-shifting filters or presets.
Slightly warm white balance.

LENS & COMPOSITION: 50mm equivalent unless noted. Shallow depth of field.
Generous off-white negative space — approximately 50% of frame as breathing
room. Single subject or two-prop maximum. Subject centred or on rule-of-thirds.

MATERIALS — all real and slightly imperfect: raw linen, unbleached muslin,
warm-taupe full-grain leather, dark wood, cream-veined off-white marble,
lime-washed walls, terracotta-toned ceramics, waxed cotton thread.

STRICTLY NO FACES. No portraits. No models above mid-calf. Hands and ankles
below the knee permitted ONLY when explicitly described. When hands appear
they are real factory workers' hands — calluses, age, real wear, henna
where it appears naturally — never aestheticised hand-model casting.

No text overlays, emojis, graphic decorations, logos, or watermarks unless
the scene explicitly requests them.

MOOD: held breath. Restraint. Slow time. An object placed down, not arranged.
The image should feel like a page from a quiet book.
```

## 10. What Comes After the Launch Nine

The launch nine is the **stance** — it teaches the audience how to read every post that follows. Steady-state content rotates through the same five pillars at roughly the same percentages, but adds:

- **Seasonal anchors** — Eid, monsoon, wedding season (always through still-life, never sale graphics)
- **Repeat below-frame humans** — ankle in motion, hand on door, walking out (never above mid-calf)
- **Material studies** — new leather batches, new lasts, new colourways introduced one shoe at a time
- **Factory windows** — quiet glimpses of production after-hours, materials at scale, finishing detail
- **Client moments** — only with explicit permission, always palette-locked, always face-absent

**What never appears, ever:**
- Faces, necks, chests, or any exposed skin above the elbow
- Immodest dress of any kind on a figure that shares the frame with the shoe
- Sale graphics, percentage discounts, or "Shop now" CTAs
- Colours outside the palette
- Emojis or exclamation marks
- All-lowercase or all-caps captions
- Fashion clichés (*iconic, must-have, drop, cop, kicks*)
- Generic shoe-brand catalogue energy
- Music on craft reels (room tone only)

The brand is built on restraint. Every time it gets loud, it loses.
