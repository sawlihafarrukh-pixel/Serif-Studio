# The Afternoon Club — Brand Notes

A product brand for people who believe in slowing down — quiet moments, intentional reflection, the kind of thinking that only happens when you give yourself time. The "afternoon" is when you pause, write, and think.

> The Afternoon Club is a space for people who believe in slowing down. For quiet moments, intentional reflection, and the kind of thinking that only happens when you give yourself time.

Brand identity is done: dusty/steel blue palette, a sun motif, refined typography pairing.

## Naming — brand vs personal name

Run two identities in parallel; they strengthen each other:

- **The Afternoon Club** — the *product* brand (journals, cards, prints, eventually a picture book). Use a brand name, not a personal name: people buy into an aesthetic and a feeling, and a brand name keeps room to grow, collaborate, or sell later.
- **Sawliha Farrukh** — the *artist/designer for hire*. Use the personal name for freelance profiles (Upwork, Contra), CV, and the personal art account.

As the brand grows, "founded by Sawliha Farrukh" becomes a CV credential; as the freelance reputation grows, it lends the brand credibility.

## Products

1. **Tadabbur Journal** — Quran-reflection journal. Structured, study-focused. See `../Tadabbur/tadabbur-journal-notes.md`. (Launch September — protected for the Pakistan sessions.)
2. **Dear Allah Diary** — personal prayer / letter-to-Allah journaling. More emotional. Distinct mood and buyer from the Tadabbur Journal — sells separately. No tie to September, so it can launch anytime (target: soft launch August).
3. **The Afternoon Journal** — a general everyday reflection journal. Cover designed but interior not started, and the cover still needs a visual anchor (texture / motif / botanical). **Leave for later** — don't spread across three journals; finish Tadabbur + Dear Allah first.
4. **Reflection cards / coasters** (concept, not yet made) — circular cards with curved-text reflection prompts that double as coasters, to help people pause mid-day. Strong first-launch candidate: physical, functional, giftable, low-cost, low-risk, on-brand, and very photogenic.
   - A set of ~10–15 prompts, e.g. "What is something I feel grateful for today?", "What made me smile today?", "What do I want to let go of?", "What am I looking forward to?", "What would make this afternoon meaningful?"
   - The only blocker was packaging — but packaging is the *easy* part and shouldn't block designing the cards. Simple options: dusty-blue rigid box or kraft sleeve with the sun motif, a cloth pouch with a tag, or a ribbon-tied stack. Design the cards first; print one locally to feel it; solve packaging after.
   - Could double as a warm-up activity in the September tadabbur sessions.

## Launch strategy

- **Anonymous soft start** is fine and smart: zero social pressure, forces the product to stand on its own, gives proof before telling anyone. Keep platforms with no obvious links back to the personal identity for now. Reveal becomes a *moment* later, not a risk.
- **Post the feeling before the product.** Build the world first — brand identity reveal, aesthetic flat lays (tea, journals, afternoon light), illustration work (the hijabi-girl-reading piece), behind-the-scenes — before selling anything.
- Discovery without an existing audience: **Pinterest** (search-driven, covers circulate for months), **TikTok/Reels** (aesthetic "journal with me" content, no face needed), and **Etsy** (its own search engine).
- On fear of pivoting publicly (Pretty Oddinary → branding page → this): pivots don't lose audiences — going silent or being inauthentic does. The thread connecting illustration + design + writing + spirituality is **convergence**, not scattered-ness.

## Reality check on income

The Afternoon Club is a slow-burn. It can make money but not quickly or reliably. Treat it as **Track 2 (income later)** built without pressure, while **Track 1 (income now)** is freelance work — see `../Career & Freelance/`. Digital products cost nothing to make or list, so the real downside risk is small.
