**Prepared by Serif Studio**
**For Kita Reka · Humankind**
**June 2026**

---

## Overview

This is a content plan for Kita Reka's Instagram. It moves the account from one-off posts toward a content **system** — a set of recurring series the studio can produce sustainably, each one extending Kita Reka's voice while protecting the studio's confidentiality commitments.

The plan is built around six concepts: two weekly recurring series, one weekly prompt format, one monthly anchor series, one one-off carousel, and one flexible-cadence series. Together they give Kita Reka 12–16 posts a month without the team starting from a blank page each time.

---

## The opportunity

The grid already has signature moments — the brushes-in-water close-up, the *"different ways to practice"* typography card, the photographic Open Studio post. These are the strongest assets in the account. The proposal builds outward from them.

A few patterns worth addressing:

- The monthly *Upcoming Workshops* cream tiles run almost identically. Strong individually, but they fatigue the grid when they sit together.
- The studio's poetic copy lands best when it stands alone, not when it's framed as a *"Did You Know?"* quiz.
- The studio's strongest visual territory is hands-and-materials close-ups — confidentiality-safe by design, and emotionally rich. There's room to lean further into this.

## Brand spec (locked)

All content sits inside Kita Reka's existing brand guide:

- **Colours:** cream `#EBE8D9`, teal `#006171`, peach `#F9B29E`, amber `#FBB144`
- **Fonts:** Butler (headers, title case, tracking −25) + Montserrat (body, sentence case, tracking 0)
- **Approved motifs:** brushes-around-palette emblem, painterly square frame, single hand with brush, giruli / scribble cluster, brush lineups
- **Hard rule:** no faces, no children in any post or video

## The six concepts

### 1. *Materials* — weekly

A close-up of one art material — what it is, what it offers, what it teaches. Cream tablecloth, natural light, hands cropped at the wrist if in frame at all.

A bank of 6–8 macros shot in a single session covers two months of weekly content.

*Why it works:* The strongest existing post (*"we saw something shift…as we let the brush lead"*) was exactly this. This series scales it.

**Sample post**
> **Watercolour.**
> For the days you want to soften the edges.

---

### 2. *The Studio at Rest* — weekly to monthly

Atmospheric photographs of the studio space with no people in frame. Before doors open. After the rinse. Golden hour. Monsoon Saturday. Soft, observational, anchoring the brand in real space.

*Why it works:* Confidentiality-safe by definition. Builds emotional texture without ever needing a person in frame.

**Sample post**
> **Before doors open.**
> The part of the day before anyone arrives.

---

### 3. *Three Materials for Three Kinds of Days* — one-off carousel

A five-slide anchor carousel: watercolour for softening, clay for holding the weight, collage for putting pieces in order. Strong candidate for a pinned post on the profile.

*Why it works:* The *"different ways to practice"* post already lands; this extends the same thinking into materials, which photograph beautifully.

---

### 4. *Found Objects* — occasional

When something interesting walks into the studio — a leaf from the carpark, a piece of string, a pressed flower — we photograph it on cream and keep a record. Quiet, generous, ownable.

*Why it works:* Ties naturally to the *Nature's Embrace* and *Grounded in Nature* lineage. Costs nothing to produce.

**Sample post**
> **Found.**
> Someone brought this in on a Wednesday. We kept it.

---

### 5. *Sunday Prompts* — weekly

A weekly typography post — one short prompt, one rotating brand colour as the background, one small motif in the corner. Invites quiet engagement without demanding DMs.

*Why it works:* Reaches people who aren't ready to come into the studio yet. Builds the *Kita Reka method* even at a distance.

**Sample prompts**
- *Draw what your shoulders feel like today.*
- *If your week was a colour, mix it.*
- *One mark for the thing you can't name yet.*

---

### 6. *Workshop Spotlights* — monthly per programme

Replace the repeating cream *Upcoming Workshops* template with workshop-specific spotlights — each one leads with the **activity**, not the poster. A macro of layered Process Painting marks. A blackout-poetry page mid-redaction. Thread and linen for Sewing Circle. A dried flower for Growing with Grief.

*Why it works:* Breaks the template fatigue. Each workshop earns its own piece of content.

## What we'd need from Kita Reka

1. **One half-day studio shoot** — covers the Materials bank, the Studio at Rest stock, and the *Three Materials* carousel macros. Around 3 hours, no models, natural light only.
2. **A "carry-the-camera" habit** for incidental Studio at Rest and Found Objects shots. A phone is fine.
3. **One naming sign-off session** for the recurring series titles, so we lock them across captions and hashtags.
4. **A 30-minute call** to discuss bilingual options — there are natural moments where Bahasa Malay would land (especially in Sunday Prompts and the brand's *kita / reka / giruli* lineage), and we'd want guidance from the team.

---

## What Serif Studio will deliver

- **Two template files** (Materials post + Sunday Prompts) in Figma or Canva, that the team can update weekly in under 10 minutes each.
- **A starter bank**: 4 Materials posts, 4 Studio at Rest posts, 3 Found Objects, 6 Sunday Prompts, 4 Workshop Spotlights, and the full *Three Materials* carousel. A 6–8 week head start.
- **A simple production schedule** the team can run from.
- **All assets** supplied in editable source files (Figma / .ai / .png) and final-export 1:1, 4:5, and 9:16 sizes.
