# Kita Reka — Studio Shoot Brief

**Prepared by Serif Studio**
**For Kita Reka · Humankind**

---

## At a glance

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | PARC Subang Jaya studio |
| **Duration** | Half-day (≈ 3 hours) |
| **Crew** | 1 photographer + 1 hand model (any adult, wrist crops only) |
| **Output** | 18–20 final frames covering Materials, Studio at Rest, and Workshop Spotlights |
| **Hard rule** | No faces, no children, no recognisable participants — ever |

---

## Setup

- **Surface.** Cream tablecloth or cream paper roll, matching brand cream `#EBE8D9`. Lay flat, no wrinkles.
- **Light.** Natural window light only. No flash. Turn off all overhead fluorescents.
- **Best windows.** Morning 9–11 am for soft directional light. Golden hour 5:30–6:45 pm for warm low-angle. Skip midday.
- **Camera.** DSLR or mirrorless preferred. Phone fine for the Studio at Rest stock. Macro lens (60–100 mm) for Materials and Workshop Spotlights.
- **Tripod.** Yes for Studio at Rest (slower shutter in low light) and for all macros.
- **Avoid.** Plastic clutter in frame, branded labels on bottles or scissors, fluorescent room light contamination, midday hard shadow.

## Block A · Materials bank — 6 macros

Each macro becomes one weekly *Materials* post. Shoot one per material, ~15 minutes each. All overhead unless noted, all on the cream surface.

### Shot 1 — Watercolour
- **Subject.** A single loaded brush touching wet cold-press paper, pigment bleeding outward in a soft halo.
- **Frame.** Overhead, very tight macro. Brush enters from upper-left. Pigment bleed dominates the lower 60 %.
- **Light.** Side window, soft left light.
- **Materials in frame.** One wood-handled round brush, one square of damp cold-press paper, no water jar visible.
- **The moment.** Mid-bleed — paper is wet, pigment is still spreading. Shoot a burst.

### Shot 2 — Charcoal
- **Subject.** A vine-charcoal stick on rough paper, one finger smudging it (wrist crop).
- **Frame.** Overhead macro. Charcoal stick diagonal, finger entering from right. Smudge fan visible.
- **Light.** Slightly harder than Shot 1 — accentuates texture.
- **Materials in frame.** One stick, one sheet of rough paper, a few flecks of dust.
- **The moment.** Mid-smudge. Motion blur is OK — it adds life.

### Shot 3 — Clay
- **Subject.** A small ball of grey or terracotta clay with a single thumb-print pressed into it.
- **Frame.** Overhead macro, tight on the clay. Generous negative space.
- **Light.** Soft, raking from the right — shows the indent depth.
- **Materials in frame.** Just the clay ball. Optional: a small dish of water out of focus.
- **The moment.** The print is fresh. No second press, no smoothing.

### Shot 4 — Oil pastel
- **Subject.** Two contrasting oil-pastel strokes layered — one teal, one peach (mirrors brand colours).
- **Frame.** Overhead macro, paper fills frame.
- **Light.** Even, diffused.
- **Materials in frame.** Two pastels visible at the edge, the strokes themselves dominating.
- **The moment.** Layering complete, strokes still glossy.

### Shot 5 — Collage
- **Subject.** Torn paper scraps mid-arrangement — some kraft, some cream, some printed. Geometric but loose.
- **Frame.** Overhead, mid-distance (not macro). Show the act of arranging — one piece angled mid-air or being placed.
- **Light.** Soft front light.
- **Materials in frame.** 6–8 paper scraps, scissors optional at frame edge.
- **The moment.** Arrangement in progress, not finished.

### Shot 6 — Ink
- **Subject.** A dip pen mid-stroke on cream paper, a single line trailing.
- **Frame.** Overhead macro. Nib enters from upper right.
- **Light.** Side light, slightly underexposed for richness.
- **Materials in frame.** Pen, paper, no ink bottle visible.
- **The moment.** The line is being drawn, ink still wet.

> **Bonus if time allows.**
> Shot 7 — Pencil graphite shavings on cream.
> Shot 8 — Soft pastel dust + finger blend.

## Block B · Studio at Rest stock — 8 shots

Atmospheric, no people. Build a stock library that drips monthly across the year. Move around the studio — capture different angles, different times, different states.

### Shot 9 — Before doors open
- **Subject.** Wide shot of the studio at 8:45 am. Chairs angled to tables. Water jars clear. Paper blank.
- **Frame.** Wide, eye-level or slightly elevated. Door visible if possible.
- **Light.** Morning light landing on one table — let it dominate.

### Shot 10 — After the rinse
- **Subject.** End-of-session clean-up moment. Cloudy water jars and brushes drying on a folded cloth.
- **Frame.** Mid-distance, slight overhead angle.
- **Light.** Late-afternoon, soft and warm.

### Shot 11 — Golden hour, west window
- **Subject.** The back corner — last light landing on a half-finished painting on an easel.
- **Frame.** Medium, eye-level.
- **Light.** 6:30–6:45 pm. Wait for the sun to hit.
- **Note.** Painting on the easel can be a leftover from a workshop (no signature visible) or staged.

### Shot 12 — The window itself
- **Subject.** Window with one single object on the sill — a small vase, a stone, a brush in a jar.
- **Frame.** Vertical, the window dominates.
- **Light.** Backlit. Slight underexposure for silhouette warmth.

### Shot 13 — The brush wall
- **Subject.** Where the brushes are stored — jars, cups, hooks. The studio's tools at rest.
- **Frame.** Mid-distance, eye-level.
- **Light.** Soft.

### Shot 14 — One chair pulled out
- **Subject.** A single chair angled away from a table, materials laid out and waiting. The implication: someone's about to sit.
- **Frame.** Mid-distance, slight overhead angle.
- **Light.** Whatever the moment is — morning preferred.

### Shot 15 — Tea station / pause moment
- **Subject.** Whatever the studio's in-between space is — a kettle, mugs, a single biscuit, a teapot.
- **Frame.** Macro or close. The detail.
- **Light.** Warm side light.

### Shot 16 — Rainy / monsoon Saturday
- **Subject.** Studio interior with rain on the window. One warm lamp on. Materials waiting on the table.
- **Frame.** Medium-wide. The window must be in frame.
- **Light.** Mostly the lamp + diffused grey light from outside.
- **Note.** Only possible on a rainy day. Tag this as an opportunistic capture — keep a phone on standby.

## Block C · Workshop Spotlights — 4 hero shots

Each becomes the lead image for a *Workshop Spotlight* post. Can be shot during actual workshops (capture only materials and finished work, no participants) or staged.

### Shot 17 — Process Painting
- **Subject.** A piece of paper on an easel mid-process. Layered intuitive paint marks. No signature, no paper edge visible. The painting feels alive but unresolved.
- **Frame.** Mid-distance, slightly off-angle. Easel implied, not centred.
- **Light.** Window light.
- **The point.** *No plan, no outcome.* The image should feel mid-thought.

### Shot 18 — Blackout Poetry
- **Subject.** A printed page with words boxed in black marker. One short phrase isolated in clean rectangles. Marker resting beside.
- **Frame.** Overhead, page fills 80 % of frame.
- **Light.** Even, soft.
- **The point.** *Finding the line that was already there.* Pre-pick a page so the visible isolated phrase is poetic.

### Shot 19 — Sewing Circle
- **Subject.** Hands-free close-up. Thread, needle, and a piece of cream linen mid-stitch. Pin cushion at edge of frame.
- **Frame.** Overhead macro.
- **Light.** Soft side window.
- **The point.** *Slow stitch.* Stitches should be visible and human — not perfect.

### Shot 20 — Growing with Grief
- **Subject.** A single dried flower on a sheet of cream paper. Soft shadow. Nothing else.
- **Frame.** Overhead, generous negative space around.
- **Light.** Soft side window, slight underexposure.
- **The point.** *A space to sit with what's still here.* Quiet. No styling.

## What NOT to shoot

- Faces, children, identifiable participants — ever.
- Branded packaging — crop or remove logos on pigment tubes, scissors, etc.
- Overhead fluorescent room light contaminating natural-light shots.
- Hard midday shadow — reschedule the shot.
- Anything Pam hasn't seen on the shoot brief — check before improvising.
