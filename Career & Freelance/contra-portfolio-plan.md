Contra is portfolio-first — clients browse visuals before reading. The first image of every project must be the strongest visual.

## Presenting work well

- **Show process, not just finals** — add 1–2 slides per project (mood board, sketches, early concepts). This separates junior from strong portfolios.
- **Write a short brief per project** (2–3 sentences: problem, solution, tools).
- **Present grid posts as individual full-size images**, not as a grid — pick 6–8 strong, varied singles (flatlay, promo graphic, lifestyle, customer-facing). Add one grid screenshot at the end to prove consistency. Lead with the best visuals; avoid leading with basic promo graphics.
- **Name files properly** — "Volta-01-Logo.jpg", not "1.jpg". Add a simple one-page PDF in each folder (project name, 2–3 sentence brief, tools used).

## Framing concept / mock-brand projects

Be transparent but professional — frame as concept or self-initiated work. Employers care that you can identify a problem and solve it visually; whether the client was real is secondary at entry level. Useful phrasing: "Self-initiated concept project", "Spec work exploring…", "Fictional brief created to explore [style/industry]".

Examples:
- **Volta:** "Self-initiated brief: a concept brand for an urban electric bike company targeting young city commuters — a bold, high-energy, tech-forward identity that keeps street credibility."
- **Sophie's Overnight Oats:** "Concept brand for a small-batch breakfast business — homemade and illustrated rather than mass-produced; warm, approachable, shelf-ready."
- **Sanis:** "Self-directed branding exercise for a minimalist leather goods brand inspired by slow fashion — natural materials, quiet luxury, tactile experience."
