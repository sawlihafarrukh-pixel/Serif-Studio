# Freelance & Selling Platforms

## Fiverr — blocked

Fiverr flagged a location mismatch (previously Pakistan, now Malaysia) and said it can't reverse it. **Do not make a new account** — that risks a permanent ban. Not a big loss; Fiverr is saturated and the alternatives suit illustration/design work better.

## Platforms for finding clients

- **Contra** — portfolio-based, clients come to you, no bidding, no commission fees. Easiest starting point; set up Malaysia-based with no location issues. See `contra-portfolio-plan.md`.
- **Upwork** — more corporate but straightforward from Malaysia, strong demand for illustrators/designers. Most direct line from existing skills to money — prioritise the profile.
- **LinkedIn / We Work Remotely** — for actual remote employment (no location-verification issues like Fiverr).
- **Instagram DMs** — genuinely how many illustrators get their first commissions; the existing art account is already an asset.

## Platforms for selling digital/physical products

- **Gumroad** — free to list, ~10% per sale, works internationally. Safest immediate option for digital products.
- **Ko-fi** — free plan; sell commissions and digital downloads. 0% fees on Ko-fi Gold ($6/mo), small fee otherwise.
- **Etsy** — biggest market for Canva templates and printables, has its own search engine. Fees: $0.20 listing (renews every 4 months or on sale), 6.5% transaction, ~3% + $0.25 payment processing (≈$1 on a $10 sale). **Check Malaysia payout/bank support before investing time.**
- **Creative Market** — design-focused audience, slightly higher prices accepted.
- **Print-on-demand** (Printify+Etsy, Lulu Direct, Amazon KDP) and **surface pattern** marketplaces (Society6, Redbubble, Spoonflower) for physical products with no inventory risk.

Given the Fiverr location experience, **verify each platform's Malaysia payment support before committing time.** Gumroad is the safest immediate start.

## Canva template business (fast income with existing skills)

- Sell on **Etsy first** (most built-in traffic), then Creative Market, then own Gumroad.
- In-demand: Instagram post/story templates for small businesses, media kits, price lists, wedding invitations, vision boards, branding kits, ebook/lead-magnet templates.
- **Edge:** a real understanding of branding means templates that work as brand *systems*, not just pretty graphics — a higher-value, differentiated product.
- Process: pick one niche (Instagram-for-small-business is the easiest entry) → make a cohesive set of 10–15 → list with search-friendly titles ("Instagram Canva Templates for Small Business — Minimal Aesthetic") → build 3–4 sets in different aesthetics.
- Pricing: 10–15 design packs ≈ $7–15 USD; 30+ bundles ≈ $25–35. **Share as a Canva *template* link, not a view link**, so buyers can copy it.
