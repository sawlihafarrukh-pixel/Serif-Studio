# Resin Phone Case Workshop — Plan

Likely the **fastest** way to make money from current skills: people pay upfront, no platform fees, no shipping, no algorithms. Builds The Afternoon Club presence in real life, and workshop photos become content.

## Concept

A ~2-hour workshop where participants make their own custom pressed-flower resin phone cases to take home. Choose **resin over watercolour** — direct experience (Pretty Oddinary), a unique take-home product, higher perceived value, and almost no competition.

## Venue options

- **Cafe A — 250 RM** to host, holds 8–10 pax.
- **Cafe B — 75 RM**, but a slower cafe.

Don't dismiss the quieter cafe on foot traffic alone — a calm, intimate space can suit a workshop better. What matters is atmosphere and fit with the brand aesthetic.

- Mask: 1 = 0.26 RM

- Phone case: 1 = 1.98 RM

- Flowers: shared ≈ 3 RM

## Group size

Resin needs ventilation, space, and individual attention. For a first workshop, **6–8 people max** (8 is manageable once confident).

## Costs and pricing (using the 250 RM venue)

|  | 6 pax | 8 pax |
|---|---|---|
| Materials | ~72 RM | ~96 RM |
| Venue | 250 RM | 250 RM |
| Total costs | ~322 RM | ~346 RM |

Reasonable price for Malaysia resin workshops: **85–100 RM/person**.
- 85 RM × 8 = 680 RM → ~334 RM profit
- 100 RM × 8 = 800 RM → ~454 RM profit

**Recommended: 90 RM/person, minimum 6 to confirm the booking** — protects the workshop if it doesn't fully fill.

## Outreach message to cafes

Warm and conversational works better than a formal pitch for art cafes. On Instagram, trim it down further and follow up with details once they respond.

> Hi! My name is Sawliha and I'm a visual artist based in Malaysia. I wanted to reach out because I'd love to host a resin phone case workshop at your cafe.
>
> The workshop would run for around 2 hours where participants create their own custom pressed flower resin phone cases to take home. I've been making resin phone cases for a few years through my own brand and would love to bring that experience into a workshop setting.
>
> I think it would be a lovely fit for your space and a great way to bring people in on quieter afternoons. I'd handle all the materials, setup and guidance — you'd just provide the venue.
>
> Would you be open to discussing this further? Happy to chat over a call or meet in person.
>
> Thank you so much!
> Sawliha
