# CV & Remote Job Search

## Strengthening the CV

- **Add a summary statement at the top**, e.g. "Visual artist and designer with a BFA and hands-on experience in brand identity, social media, and digital design, seeking remote creative roles."
- **Reframe experience with impact, not tasks** — add results where possible ("Designed brand identities for X clients as a freelancer", "Grew social media presence for The Cookie Ninja bakery").
- **Highlight remote-ready tools prominently** — Procreate, Canva Pro, Illustrator, Lightroom, AI image generation. Group under "Design Tools" and add collaboration tools used (Notion, Slack, Figma, Trello).
- **Add a portfolio link** — non-negotiable for creative remote work. Behance, Adobe Portfolio, a GitHub Pages site, or a well-organised Google Drive all work. Put it directly under name/contact, labelled "Portfolio:". A Google Drive folder set to "Anyone with the link can view", organised into clean subfolders with descriptive filenames, is acceptable to start.
- **Quantify Pretty Oddinary** — how long it ran, number of products, sales milestones. Running your own brand is a strong asset; lean into the entrepreneurial angle.

### Focus the CV — pick one direction

The CV currently sits in the middle of two stories. Pick a primary:
- **Direction 1 — Graphic Designer & Brand Identity:** branding, Canva, Illustrator, AI work. Best for social media designer / brand designer / content creator roles.
- **Direction 2 — Visual Artist & Illustrator:** illustration, watercolour, calligraphy, Procreate, After Effects, with branding as commercial proof. Best for illustration commissions, children's books, editorial, cultural design.

Strongest unique combination = **Direction 2**: illustration background + digital design skills + a real brand built from scratch. Group skills by relevance rather than listing everything — a focused CV beats a scattered one.

### Under-marketed skills worth surfacing

After Effects / motion graphics (2D game concept, university project), watercolour & painting, calligraphy, traditional illustration, photography. Calligraphy and motion graphics are niche and in demand.

## Entry-level remote roles to target

- Social Media Content Creator / Coordinator
- Graphic Designer (remote freelance or junior)
- Digital Marketing Assistant
- Product Listing Designer (Etsy/Amazon sellers)
- AI Image Generation Specialist (growing niche)
- Virtual Assistant (Creative)
- Junior digital illustrator for small brands; Canva template designer

## Where to look

- Remote job boards: We Work Remotely, Remote.co, Himalayas
- LinkedIn with the "Remote" filter on entry-level creative roles
- Contra (great for creatives), Upwork (build early reviews)
- (Skip Fiverr — see `freelance-platforms.md`)

## Niche opportunities (illustration)

Children's book illustration, surface pattern design, editorial illustration, and **Muslim/Islamic illustrated content** (journals, children's books, cards, stickers, prints) — an underserved market that fits the personal style and story authentically. For children's books: build 2–3 spec pieces (a short public-domain tale or a tiny original story, 3–4 scenes, consistent Procreate style) — standard practice before a first commission.
